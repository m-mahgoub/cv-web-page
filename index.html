Mohamed Mahgoub, MD

Institute for Frontier Life and Medical Sciences, Kyoto University

I am a physician and enthusiastic biomedical scientist. During my clinical practice after getting my medical degree, I found out how much is still unknown about our own bodies, as well as our rivals in life like microbes. Therefore I decided to pursue PhD in medical virology, looking for some answers for this amazing puzzle of life; and having fun!

Experience

Molecular Biology Research

KYOTO UNIVERSITY

I have been studying the molecular mechanisms of viral oncogenesis by using HTLV-1 (Human T-cell leukemia virus type 1) as a model. My focus on how the virus can establish latency and fine-tune its own gene expression to simultaneously evade immunity as well as manipulate transcriptome of hosting cells to promote cancer. I use cutting edge molecular techniques to monitor viral and host gene expression at single cell level.
